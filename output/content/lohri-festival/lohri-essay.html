History & Significance

1

The Legend of Dulla Bhatti

Lohri is well-known for the story of Dulla Bhatti, also known as the 'Robin Hood of Punjab', who saved girls from being sold into slavery and ensured they had dignified marriages with dowries.

2

Harvest Connection

This festival signals the conclusion of peak winter and aligns with the Rabi crop cycle of wheat and mustard, honoring the dedication of farmers and the anticipation of a bountiful harvest.

3

The Holy Bonfire

During Lohri, the bonfire represents Agni and the sun's revival, as people gather around to offer sesame, jaggery, and peanuts in hopes of prosperity.

A Song of Dulla Bhatti

"Sundri-Mundri hoye! Tera kaun vicharaa! Dulla Bhatti wala!"

During Lohri, the hero's memory is kept alive as this well-loved folk song resonates in every neighborhood.
